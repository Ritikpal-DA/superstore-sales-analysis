# Superstore Sales Performance Analysis

## About This Project
I built this project to practise real-world data analysis skills.
I took a publicly available retail dataset with nearly 10,000 orders,
wrote SQL queries to dig into the data, and then visualised the findings
in an Excel dashboard — all from scratch.

## Tools I Used
- **SQL** — PostgreSQL (pgAdmin4) for querying and analysis
- **Microsoft Excel** — for the dashboard, charts, and formatting

## About the Dataset
- **Source:** Kaggle — Sample Superstore Dataset
- **Size:** 9,994 rows and 21 columns
- **Includes:** Order dates, regions, product categories, sales,
  profit, discounts, customer names, and more

## Questions I Tried to Answer
1. What is the overall sales and profit of the business?
2. Which region is bringing in the most revenue?
3. Which product category is actually making the most profit?
4. Who are the top 5 customers by total purchases?
5. Which sub-category is giving away the most discount?
6. Which state is placing the most orders?
7. How are the three customer segments performing?
8. Which year was the best for sales?
9. Which orders are running at a loss?
10. Which region has the best profit per order on average?

## What I Found
- **West region** leads in revenue with ₹7,25,457 in total sales
- **Technology** is the most profitable category — ₹1,45,455 in profit
- **Consumer segment** accounts for over 50% of total sales
- **2017** was the strongest year with ₹7,33,215 in sales
- **Sean Miller** is the top customer with ₹25,043 in total purchases
- Some orders are running at a **loss due to high discounts** — a risk worth flagging
